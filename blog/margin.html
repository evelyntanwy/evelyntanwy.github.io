Margin vs Padding vs Border

Why do we use them differently

In CSS Coding world when we need to align a certain image, text or item you will come across hearing these words margin, padding or border. But what do they mean? On a webpage, they are segregated into different sections shown below.

Margin

Margin is the space around an element's border. In a simple form the margin property controls the space outside the element. When we write margin : 10px; it means that there will be 10 pixels space between the element and adjacent page element. The margin also pushes its neighboring elements which allows them to have some room to breath.

Padding

As for padding, it is located inside the border of an element to generate space. In order to create a gap the padding either grows the element's size or shrinks the content.

Border

Border surrounds the elements that can be modified in various ways like solid, dashed or even rounded corners.

In short, margin is the space around an element's border, padding is the space between an element's border.
