Problem solving techniques and process

• Pseudocode

I really like this method, as it helps me plan the flow and thought process of coding. It helps me when I get stuck to try to work my way down. I guess this is something I do on a daily basis when I plan to cook, checking my ingredient and to prep the food accordingly.

• Trying something

During the challenges, I usually just try something at the start and depending on what the results return. Sometime its correct and I move on to to trying the next, other times when an error message shows I will try to work them out.

• Rubber ducky method

Recently I realised I have been reading and speaking about my problem out loud and by doing that I was able to pin point my issue and slowing it. The rubber ducky method is a great way to analyse the problem.

• Reading error messages

I am still learning to read error messages and understanding the analogy of each term. But the error message for syntax error is a great help to me for making my code work.

• Console.logging

Prior to Week 4 we were introduced to console.loging. But during week 4 sprint I have new found love for console.logging. It shows me where Im at and where I’m needed to move forward with the code. Just by coding you don’t actual know if you are on the right path, whereas with console.logging you get to see what is printed out and if you are on the right track.

• Googling

This is by far the most used and one of the most efficient way. I literally google everything and would make it an effort to do for awhile before I seek help with a problem.

• Asking your peers for help

I need to work on this. At times when Im stuck I’m not sure what the problems is, therefore its is hard for me to ask a questions not knowing what my questions is. This is def something Im trying, asking for help when needed.

• Asking coaches for help

The coaches are wonderful with their insight and understanding. I’m comfortable asking them for help and helping me with my problems.

• Improving your process with reflection

I have yet to properly sit down and reflect on my problems or mistake, as I tend to just try to finish through the challenges and complete them. It would be something I would like to work on it and do more. This would def benefit me in the future.
